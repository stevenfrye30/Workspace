# Kena Upanishad

## Primary Text

F. Max Müller, *The Upanishads, Part I*, Sacred Books of the East, Vol. I (1879). Public domain via SacredTexts.com. Khanda 1, verses 1–4 — the opening pupil-teacher dialogue establishing the *X-of-X* formula and the categorical denial of cognitive/linguistic access to Brahman.

**Khanda 1, verse 1.** The Pupil asks: 'At whose wish does the mind sent forth proceed on its errand? At whose command does the first breath go forth? At whose wish do we utter this speech? What god directs the eye, or the ear?'

**Khanda 1, verse 2.** The Teacher replies: 'It is the ear of the ear, the mind of the mind, the speech of speech, the breath of breath, and the eye of the eye. When freed (from the senses) the wise, on departing from this world, become immortal.

**Khanda 1, verse 3.** 'The eye does not go thither, nor speech, nor mind. We do not know, we do not understand, how any one can teach it.

**Khanda 1, verse 4.** 'It is different from the known, it is also above the unknown, thus we have heard from those of old, who taught us this.
