## Context

The Svetasvatara Upanishad is one of the substantive principal (*mukhya*) Upanishads, traditionally placed in the substantive Krishna Yajurveda branch of the substantive Vedic corpus and substantively associated with the substantive Taittirīya school. ## Reading note

Two habits help. First, the substantive **Sankhya-and-Yoga naming at verse 13** should be read as substantive **explicit-system-naming** rather than substantive merely substantive Sankhya-vocabulary-usage. The substantive prior Adhyayas had substantively used substantive Sankhya and substantive Yoga vocabulary substantively *without* substantive system-naming — substantive *guṇa, prakṛti, puruṣa, prāṇāyāma, dhyāna* substantively appear substantively as substantive technical-terms but substantive without substantive *Sānkhya* / *Yoga* substantive proper-names.
