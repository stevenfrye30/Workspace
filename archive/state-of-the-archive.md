# State of the archive

This page records, in plain numbers, where the archive stands today.
It is updated when the underlying corpus is re-validated, not on a
schedule.

*Last reviewed: 2026-05-10.*

## Reading Room

The curated public archive — texts edited to be read.

- **205** entries prepared.
- **A small number** sit on the front shelf at any given time.
- The rest live in the **[deeper shelves](shelves.html)**, organised by tradition.

The Reading Room is intentionally not a complete library. It is a
small, careful room. Texts arrive when they are read, and rise to the
front shelf when they are returned to often.

## The corpus behind it

A larger, schema-validated corpus stands behind the Reading Room and
supports the daily reading service.

- **~1,091** distinct texts.
- **~1,200** translations.
- **22,651** raw source files cryptographically anchored.
- **99.88%** of reader-facing passages verified verbatim against their
  raw sources, letter for letter.

The Reading Room is hand-authored. It is not a generated mirror of the
corpus. The corpus is comprehensive; the Reading Room is curated. They
are deliberately separate surfaces, sharing care.

## Discipline

Each Reading Room entry follows a simple discipline: the source
translation is named, the verses are reproduced verbatim from that
source, and the archive's own commentary is kept clearly separate from
the text it discusses. The full convention is set out in
[STANDARD.md](STANDARD.md).

A small number of pilot entries follow a stricter version of that
standard, with explicit visual separation between primary text and
commentary. Migration of the rest is incremental and unhurried.

## What this is

A long-term project. Texts arrive slowly. Numbers above will move, but
not quickly. The archive is built to be read, not to be finished.
